About :-
The Elephanta Caves, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, are a group of ancient rock-cut caves located on Elephanta Island in Mumbai Harbor, Maharashtra, India. These caves are renowned for their elaborate Hindu and Buddhist rock-cut sculptures and artistry.
Brief History: -
The Elephanta Caves are believed to have been created during the rule of the Kalachuri dynasty in the 6th to 7th centuries CE. However, the exact origins and history of the caves are not entirely clear, and there are several theories about their creation.
