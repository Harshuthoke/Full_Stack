About :-
The Aga Khan Palace is a historical monument located in Pune, Maharashtra, India. It holds great significance in the Indian freedom struggle and is closely associated with Mahatma Gandhi and the Quit India Movement.
Brief History: -
The Aga Khan Palace, located in Pune, Maharashtra, India, was commissioned by Sultan Muhammed Shah Aga Khan III, who was the 48th Imam of the Ismaili Shia Muslims. The palace was built in 1892 with the primary aim of providing employment to the famine-stricken local population.
Entry Fee :-
9:30 AM – 5:30 PM (Daily)
Rs 5 (Locals) & Rs 100 (Foreign)
