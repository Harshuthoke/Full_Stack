About :-
Pratapgad Fort, also known as Pratapgarh Fort, is a historic hill fortress located in the Western Ghats of Maharashtra, India. It holds great historical significance due to its association with the Maratha warrior king Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj and the Battle of Pratapgad
Brief History: -
Pratapgad was built in 1656 under the orders of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. The construction of the fort was strategically significant as it was intended to protect the Maratha kingdom from potential enemy invasions, especially from the Adil Shahi dynasty of Bijapur.
Pratapgad gained immense historical importance during the Battle of Pratapgad in 1659. In a legendary confrontation, Shivaji defeated and killed Afzal Khan, which not only marked a significant military victory but also established Shivaji's reputation as a formidable warrior and leader
